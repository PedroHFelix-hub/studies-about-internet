HOW DID THE INTERNET EMERGE?

STUDENT: Pedro Felix

War Context

The internet was born out of the need to connect computers and share information. In the context of the Cold War, the United States Department of Defense created ARPANET in 1969 — a computer network that enabled communication between academic and military institutions. Over time, ARPANET evolved and expanded, eventually giving rise to the internet as we know it today: a global network connecting billions of devices, allowing the exchange of information, communication, and access to a wide range of online services.

ARPANET

Inspired by the launch of the Sputnik satellite, scientists developed ARPANET as a way to ensure resilient communication in case of enemy attacks. ARPANET used a technology called packet switching, which divided information into small packets sent through different routes — ensuring communication even if part of the network was compromised.

At that time, communication between computers was still limited, but ARPANET established the foundations for the modern internet. It continued to grow, connecting universities, military units, researchers, and commercial organizations — each running different types of computers, which made communication a major engineering challenge.

PROTOCOLS AND COMMUNICATION

To enable connections between different machines, computers had to follow specific communication rules called protocols. ARPANET initially used the NCP (Network Control Protocol), but it had significant limitations.

LIMITATIONS OF NCP

  • Instability: The protocol was unreliable and prone to failures.
  • Lack of Interoperability: It could not communicate with other networks, whether research or commercial.
  • Single "Language": NCP worked like a translator that understood only one language, preventing integration with different systems.

SOLUTION — TCP/IP

  • Developed by Vinton Cerf and Bob Kahn to overcome NCP's limitations.
  • Introduced universal communication between different networks and systems worldwide.
  • In 1983, its adoption officially marked the birth of the modern internet.
  • TCP/IP remains the foundational protocol suite of the internet to this day.

FROM ARPANET TO THE MODERN INTERNET

  • 1969: ARPANET is created with just 4 nodes.
  • 1971: First email is sent over ARPANET.
  • 1983: TCP/IP is adopted — the modern internet is born.
  • 1990: ARPANET is officially shut down after fulfilling its purpose.
  • 1991: Tim Berners-Lee introduces the World Wide Web (WWW).
  • 1990s: Dial-up internet spreads to homes using telephone lines and modems.
  • 2000s: Broadband, Wi-Fi, and mobile networks bring faster and more flexible access.
  • Today: Over 5 billion people are connected to the internet globally.
